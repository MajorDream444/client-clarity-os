# Creator-Healer Intake

Use this for Theo-style clients: founder-led healing, coaching, transformation work, personal doctrine, visibility resistance, paid proof, and community pathways.

## Opening

This intake is here to capture the real shape of your work before anyone tries to package, price, automate, or scale it.

Answer in your own language. Short, honest answers are better than polished ones.

## Origin

- What life experience, wound, study, practice, or calling shaped this work?
- What did you have to live through or learn before you could hold this space for others?
- What language do clients already use when they describe your impact?

## Work / Authority

- What do you help people move through, unlock, restore, or become?
- What methods, rituals, frameworks, or practices do you actually use?
- Where do you already have trust, credibility, or proof?

## People / ICP

- Who is this work truly for right now?
- What are they carrying before they find you?
- What are they secretly hoping is possible?
- Who is not a fit, even if they could pay?

## Offer / Revenue

- What has already made money?
- What do people ask you for most often?
- What feels easiest to deliver with integrity?
- What feels underpriced, unclear, or too manually held?

## Resistance / Boundaries

- Where do you feel resistance to visibility, selling, structure, or being seen?
- What kind of growth would feel misaligned?
- What boundaries must the business protect?

## Expression / Delivery

- How do you most naturally teach, speak, host, guide, or transmit?
- What should become a front door?
- What might become a community, workshop, retreat, or deeper pathway later?

## Vision

- If the work was held by a simple system, what would become possible?
- What should exist in a year that does not need to be built in the first 30 days?

## Reality Check

- What audience, list, relationships, assets, testimonials, or offers already exist?
- What is missing before this can reliably sell?
- What should not be built yet?

## Final Reflection

What is the simplest first build that would let the right people understand the work, trust the path, and take the next step?
